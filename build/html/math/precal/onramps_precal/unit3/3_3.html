3.3 Growth and Decay¶

3.3.1 Exponential Functions¶

There are 3 common types of Exponential Functions:

  1. \(y = Ce^{kt}\) - General Growth Model

  2. \(y = y_0 + Ce^{kt}\) - Newton’s Law of Cooling

  3. \(y = \frac{L}{1 + Ce^{-kLt}}\) - General Logistic Model

General Growth Model Functions:¶

The General Growth Model can be used in many day-to-day situations, but it assumes that the function grows without limits. This Model is not accurate when dealing with populations.

Below are some of the most common Exponential Functions that model Growth or Decay situations.

General Exponential¶

\(y = a(b)^{x}\)

Percent Increase/Decrease¶

\(y = a(1 \pm r)^{kt}\)

Arguments:

  • \(a\) - Initial Amount

  • \(r\) - Rate of Increase/Decrease

  • \(k\) - Number of Increases/Decreases per year

  • \(t\) - Time

Compound Interest Compounded at Regular Intervals¶

\(A = P \lgroup 1 + \frac{r}{n} \rgroup^{nt}\)

Arguments:

  • \(A\) - Final Amount

  • \(P\) - Initial Amount

  • \(r\) - APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

  • \(n\) - Number of compounds per year

  • \(t\) - Time

Compound Interest Compounded Continuously¶

\(A = Pe^{rt}\)

Arguments:

  • \(A\) - Final Amount

  • \(P\) - Initial Amount

  • \(r\) - APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

  • \(t\) - Time

Half-Life¶

\(N(t) = N_0e^{\frac{-t\ln2}{h}}\)

Arguments:

  • \(N(t)\) - Final Amount

  • \(N_0\) - Initial Amount

  • \(t\) - Time

  • \(h\) - Half-Life Time

Logistic Growth Model¶

The Logistic Growth Model is well adapted to modeling situations where the growth of the independent variable is limited.

Logistic Function¶

\(y = \frac{L}{1 + Ce^{-kLt}}\)

Arguments:

  • \(L\) - The Curve’s Maximum Value, Carrying Capacity

  • \(C, k, L\) - Constants

  • \(t\) - Time
